My name is Jade Kelly and I'm a Seattle-based developer who specializes in front-end development and design.

I love crafting experiences. I love a challenging puzzle, and the satisfaction that comes from solving it. I love experimenting, and I'm not afraid to track down a fix if I try something that breaks my project. I love seeing the power of programming at work, and harnessing it.

I've been cheerfully exploring the ins and outs of web development ever since I realized anybody could make a website. At the tender age of ten, I asked how I could make a website, and I was pointed toward the now-defunct MySpace. This sated me only temporarily--I refused to be fettered by the site's limitations. From there, it was HTML tutorials, tracking down free ways to host, diving into forum moderation and development. I loved it all!

These days, I don't have the time for developing or moderation of such social platforms, but I do enjoy coming up with templates and themes for others to use. I've also dabbled in other forms of experience-crafting -- applications and video games are particularly exciting to me. The education I've received in these fields is invaluable to me; it's a wonder what can be accomplished just by harnessing language in the right way.

When I'm not playing with front-end adjacent hobbies, there are numerous other things I enjoy doing to spend my time. I write fiction, I play video games, I read, I study psychology, I design for-print media. And it's my belief that these activities, too, lend to my abilities and enrich me as an individual and as a professional.

I aspire to learn and grow, to expand my horizons beyond that which is most familiar to me.

At this time, my knowledge as a programmer may be focused toward the client-side, but I am equally intrigued by back-end, and aspire to grow my skills in full-stack work.
